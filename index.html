Jamey Helsman (Judas)

In the fall of 1994, college freshman baseball player, I auditioned for Midnight Productions’ Jesus Christ Superstar in the community room of the Oxford Valley Mall—which, if you’ve ever been there, is exactly as glamorous as it sounds. What I didn’t know then was that one audition would bring me into a family that’s been stuck with me for over 30 years. Somewhere along the way, castmates turned into lifelong friends… and now you’re all basically relatives whether you like it or not. A lot of us grew up on the Keswick stage—learning our notes, missing our cues, and pretending we knew what we were doing. We’ve shared comedy, tragedy, and a whole lot of “what just happened?” moments. For me, nothing tops getting to share that stage with my two daughters, Smooch and Bubba. That was pretty special. We did try to get my son E up there too… but he made the decision to stay in the audience and judge us from a safe distance. I love you three more than anything. For the last 20-some years, I’ve been down in North Carolina—same sales job since 2007, coaching high school baseball since 2015. Every now and then my two passions collide, and they let me sing the National Anthem… which either inspires a crowd or makes them wish the game would start sooner. A huge thank you to my family, extended family, and friends for supporting this show all these years. Seriously, the fact that you keep coming back means either you love us… or you just don’t know how to say no. To Newt and Alan—I don’t know how you’ve put up with me this long, but at this point, I think it’s legally binding. I love you guys. To Mom—none of this happens without you. Thank you for being my rock, my biggest supporter, and probably my longest-running fan. And to Aunt Peggy—thanks for the 20 bucks and the gummy bears. Honestly, still one of the best sponsorship deals I’ve ever had. .

Production Team

Diane Wargo (Director)

Diane Wargo has been active in theatre for over 50 years as an actress, director, choreographer, teacher, and producer. She is thrilled to return to the staging of Jesus Christ Superstar. A speech and theatre major, Diane earned degrees from Mercer County Community College, Trenton State College, and Villanova University, and furthered her training studying with Stella Adler in New York City. She has been a member of the Performing Arts Department at Notre Dame High School for 40 years, where she teaches Acting and Public Speaking and directs the spring production. Her favorite directing and choreography credits include Bat Boy: The Musical, Oklahoma!, The Adventures of Tom Sawyer, Brigadoon, Our Town, Man of La Mancha, Once on This Island, Fiddler on the Roof, Peter Pan, Radium Girls, and numerous original and immersive productions. As a performer, she has toured and appeared in leading roles, including Ado Annie (Oklahoma!), Sonia (Godspell), Hermia (A Midsummer Night’s Dream), Meg Brockie (Brigadoon), and Annie Oakley in Annie Get Your Gun. Diane has also worked with Midnight Productions for over 20 years and serves as Cast Manager for Sleepy Hollow Haunted Acres in Newtown, PA each haunt season. She is grateful to share her passion for theatre with the cast, crew, and musicians—and sends special thanks to her mom and Chelsea for their constant love and support.

Miranda Leiggi has a BA in theatre from Drew University, and an MFA in creative writing from Arcadia University. She has worked as a scenic artist, set designer, costume designer, and makeup artist locally, Off-Broadway and with NJ SWAT and FBI. A series of short films she worked on was featured at San Diego Comic Con. Miranda is the director for the theater program at Council Rock High School North, and is currently directing Sweeney Todd.
